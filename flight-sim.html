Flight Simulator

After growing increasingly frustrated with the game "War Thunder" for its predatory pricing model and uninspired gameplay, I decided to try my hand at making my own flight simulator game. I'm still far from finished, but I think what I have so far is worthy of sharing regardless.

My initial goal for the game was to make a generalized flight model that behaves realistically while being simple enough to quickly tune to many different airframes. I ended up using aspects from a technique I found in a video by Jump Trajectory that simulates the forces acting on each lifting surface of the aircraft individually. In the video, Jump Trajectory also describes a method of dynamically calculating the coefficients of lift and drag for a wing surface, but I found that it was easier to create the lift/drag coefficient curves by hand and storing them as a Unity AnimationCurve. I based the shape of the coefficient curves on whatever publicly available data I could find on the aircraft. In the future, I'd like to do more research on other techniques

The lift and drag calculations are based on the equations for coefficients of lift and drag.
